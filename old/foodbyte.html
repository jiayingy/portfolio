The idea of FoodByte is such that it works like an online food delivery platform like Deliveroo and Food Panda. It will allow users, who are not able to order enough food items to hit the minimum order amount for delivery, to consolidate their orders with other people in the vincinity. FoodByte will allow users to track how much more orders are needed before their food gets delivered.

How did FoodByte come about?

I often have to stay late in school to finish up projects and assignments when it is nearing the submission period. However, school canteens would close around 6:30pm - 7:00pm. After this time, our only options would be to get food from vending machines or to call for delivery. But delivery would come with a minimum order amount and delivery fee. If I'm in school alone, how will I be able to meet the minimum order?
Thus the idea of FoodByte was born!

What I did?

I came up with survey questions for students and interview questions for lecturers who often stay late in school to understand their pain points when getting food after canteens have closed.

I also conducted contextual inquiry with a few students to understand how they use the current food delivery web applications.

From the interviews and survey results, some of the findings are:

  • Some are conscious about the type of food they eat and they find it hard to find healthier foods in school after canteens have closed
  • Students/Lecturers want to get their work done quickly thus unwilling to travel out of school to get food during this timing
  • Even when they want to order delivery, they would be unsure of what to eat sometimes

From the findings, I then turned it into user needs to adddress the issues above:

  • The app should include a calories counter for the health conscious group
  • The app should allow users to consolidate their food with other people in the vincinity
  • The app should give suggestions to the users on what to eat

After researching on user needs, I did a storyboard on the to understand the flow of users when searching for food in school after canteens have closed.
